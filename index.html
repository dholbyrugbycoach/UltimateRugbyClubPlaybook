Pillars of Success

The five foundations on which everything we do is built

Key Values — Discipline · Respect · Loyalty. These are the minimum standard to wear this jersey — not aspirations.
💪 Pillar 1 — Be the Fittest Team in the Competition
Tight 5Strength and running fitness. The engine room of everything. Must be able to dominate physically for the full 80 minutes.
Middle 5Strength, speed and agility. Link the forwards and backs — need to cover ground fast and hit hard at the breakdown.
Outside 5Strength and linear speed. Must be able to create and finish. Fit enough to be threats 0–100m all game.

Be able to outperform opposition in the last 30 minutes of any game. That is when games are decided and when our fitness becomes our weapon.

⚡ Pillar 2 — Be the Best Attacking Team (0–100m)

Win the gain line and pass the ball to attack their weakness. Keep the defence on the back foot — don't let them recover. Always look up — make a quick decision.

A
Shape gives us options. RED (Bull — direct off 9) · GREEN (Shark — wide off 9) · WHITE (X — cross off 10). All three options live simultaneously every phase. Balance is the key.
B
Linkage — 9, 10, 12 on a string. These three work together always — presenting RED, GREEN and WHITE simultaneously. One movement forces the defence to make a choice.
C
Reloading. Hard on effort — get to your feet and move quickly to where you are most effective next. Tight 5 reload to RED, back row to GREEN, backs to WHITE. 15 on feet in attack shape at all times.
D
Set piece and first phase work as one. A dominant set piece creates the platform. The gain line must be won in first phase to allow the attack to roll on with momentum.
🛡️ Pillar 3 — Stop the Ball (4 vs 2)

Create a 4 vs 2 numerical advantage at every breakdown. Four defenders committed to stopping the ball against two attackers — this is the target at every ruck.

Urgency with Control, Communication, Spacing and Line speed are essential. All players must be active in getting into the defensive line and reacting on call — UP.

WIND Reaction A physical and mental change of speed and intent. The moment we win the ball back, we attack immediately. Stop the opposition attack in the first 20 seconds after any turnover. WIND = thunder — go now.
💥 Pillar 4 — Breakdown Fundamentals
Ball Grip
Squeeze before contact. Never go to ground without full control.
Ball Position
Present toward own players. Make 9's life easy.
TK Speed
Squeeze the ball — low and fast to the breakdown.
4th Man
Read the picture — make the right decision.
2
2nd man: Take space or take the threat. Read immediately on arrival — is the ball secure or is there a threat to clear? Decide and commit. One bullet.
3
3rd man: Secure the inside gate — always. This is non-negotiable. No exceptions. The gate must be secured before anything else. One kill.
4
4th man: Make a decision. Is the breakdown secure? Is there a threat on the edge? Is there space to exploit? Be the smart player — read and react immediately.
🎯 Pillar 5 — Set Piece: Technically Superior
ScrumSet low — technically superior to every opponent. Win the hit every engagement. Quickest and cleanest delivery in our competition. Hooker's responsibility to win the hit speed.
LineoutGreater attention to detail than any team we face. Understand momentum. Always quicker than the opposition — tempo is our weapon. Precise delivery damages opposition confidence.
Kick-OffImprove fundamentals every week. Contest aerially. Apply pressure to their kicker. Smart attack options on our kick-off — never just kick for territory and hope.

Pitch Map

Field zones · Attack decisions · Gain line priority · Kicking strategy

Zone Priorities
🟡 Gold Zone — Strike

Win the gain line and attack their weakness immediately. Keep the defence on the back foot. Must come away with points. Shape gives us options — make the right read, go in first phase. Set piece and first phase work as one here — no wasted possessions.

🟢 Green Zone — Build

Shape–Linkage–Gain Line–Quick Ball. Always look up and make quick decisions. 9/10/12 on a string, all options live simultaneously. 15 on feet in attack shape at all times. Tight 5 reload to RED, back row to GREEN, backs to WHITE.

🟠 Amber Zone — Territory

Territory before points. Gain the gain line or kick for territory. 3 phases — if no gain line advantage, apply pressure through BLACK (kick). Running plays must create forward momentum — no lateral play without purpose.

🔴 Red Zone — Exit

Exit fast. Minimise rucks in own 22. Kick long and contest their lineout. Running only if certain of gain line. No lateral plays under pressure in our own half. Every second we spend here is a gift to the opposition.

Attack — Shape

Win the gain line · Attack their weakness · RED · GREEN · WHITE

Always look up — make a quick decision. Shape gives us options. Keep the defence on the back foot — never let them recover. Heads up rugby, 0–100m.
⚡ The Three Bases — RED · GREEN · WHITE
🔴 RED — Bull (Direct)9 direct carry. F1 set on outside shoulder of D1, attacks the D1/D2 gap. On RED, 9 runs. Extra player from ruck fills pocket of 10 or 9 — GOLD double up. Tight 5 reload to F1.
🟢 GREEN — Shark (Wide)9 fires wide to F2. F2 cuts past D3/D4 — attack the edge. Happens simultaneously with RED, forcing the defence to choose. Back row reloads to GREEN. Shark cuts and goes.
⚪ WHITE — X (Cross)Late ball to 10. F3 crosses behind the pod — attacks the gap created by RED and GREEN movement pulling defenders inside. Backs reload to WHITE. Spare forward fills 10/9 pockets.
📋 Attack Decision Making — ROK
R
Read. Always look up before receiving the ball. Identify which defender is most vulnerable — D1, D2, D3, D4. See the defence shape before the ball arrives.
O
Options. Know your three options before the ball is played. RED (D1/D2 gap) · GREEN (edge) · WHITE (cross). Which one gives us the best chance to win the gain line?
K
Kill. Execute with commitment and pace. Go directly through the read, not sideways. Every carry must have a target. Win the gain line — attack their weakness.
📋 Reloading — Who Goes Where
T5
Tight 5 reload to RED. After the breakdown, get to your feet and move to the F1 carrying position. Support the direct carry line.
BR
Back Row reloads to GREEN. Get to the edge position — F2 in the GREEN line. Always a wide threat after every breakdown.
BK
Backs reload to WHITE. 10/12/13 restructure the cross option. 15 must always be on feet in attack shape — the safety valve and extra option.

Linkage & Reload

9/10/12 on a string · 15 on feet · Kick return structure · Kiji X Shuttle

🔗 Linkage — 9, 10, 12 on a String

9, 10 and 12 must always work together. RED, GREEN and WHITE are always in motion simultaneously — presenting options to the defence at all times.

9
9 controls RED and GREEN simultaneously. A glance left presents RED (F1) — a glance right presents GREEN (F2). The defence must respect both before the ball is played.
10
10 controls the RED structure and WHITE. Manages F1, F2 in the RED base. If in the ruck — 12 takes the 10 role. The WHITE option always runs through 10.
12
12 controls GREEN (minimum F3). If 10 is occupied, 12 takes over the RED structure. If 12 is in the ruck, the next back takes the WHITE role.
🔄 Kick Return — TAIHO (Reload After Catch)

TAIHO. After catching a kick — reload straight back into our attack shape. Read kick cues early (in the pocket) and move before the ball arrives. 13 and open wing typically have 5 back.

1
Pass, Run or Kick options immediately. Ball carrier into contact only if it is 1 vs 1 — TK. Closest 3 players clean out. Then reload to shape.
2
10 controls the RED structure (F1 and F2). 12 controls GREEN (minimum F3). Aim is linkage of RED/GREEN/WHITE — look at space immediately.
3
If 10 is in the ruck — 12 takes the 10 role. If 12 is in the ruck — the next back takes the WHITE role. Chain of responsibility never breaks.
🦵 Kicking Structure — Brilliant Simple Kicking
Left Hand Side (LHS)From LHS set piece — Sora delivery (kick from 10). 10 kicks on receipt from 9. Kick return: 10 recovers LHS. Kick to contest, then reload.
Right Hand Side (RHS)From RHS set piece — Katame (kick from 9). 9 kicks direct. Kick return: 9 recovers RHS. Efficient, no wasted movement.
Slow Ball — Kiji XWhen ruck ball is slow — Kiji X Shuttle. Shape adjusts to create time and space. Backs flood into width. 15 safety position. Re-establish shape before attacking.

Defence System

4 vs 2 · Ruck structure 1,1,2,3 · STORM · TIDE · MIST · WIND

4 vs 2. Create a numerical advantage at every breakdown. WIND — physical and mental change of speed. Stop their attack in the first 20 seconds after every turnover. UP.
📋 Basic Structure — Ruck Defence 1, 1, 2, 3
Defender 1 (1m from ruck)Sets first on the immediate side of the breakdown. Defends inside ball — defends the inside runner if 9 runs at Defender 2. Not moved out of position by players folding. Sets both sides. Never vacated.
Defender 2 (hits 9)Defends the channel between half and 10. Hits halfback if they run. 9 is positioned behind this defender on defence.
Defender 3 (outside 10)Defends outside shoulder of 10. Can drift after 2nd pass. Must continue to move forward throughout.
All DefendersUrgency with Control, Communication, Spacing and Line speed are essential. Must be active in getting into the defensive line and reacting on call — UP.
⚡ STORM — Smash (Blitz)

Fast line speed, led up and called by the outside men. Implemented when there is an obvious numbers advantage, slow ruck ball, or strategically from set piece.

When to useSeeing T5 players only or 10 can only play short. Led from the outside in — outside men initiate and all follow.
🌊 TIDE — Default (Hot)

Default defensive system — used every phase. First 3 steps are hard off the line.

Line speed patternQuick – slow – quick. Off the line hard. Slow to assess. Then hard again on 2nd pass. Gachi defence on the inside. Creates contested, slow ball — reload.
🌫️ MIST — Cold (Shortage)

Called by wide players on shortage of defenders — especially on WIND (turnover) ball. No defenders move forward on MIST call.

SequenceBack off — give immediate gain line. Once spacing achieved, all slide across and force attack to sideline. When numbers equal — go forward. Next phase: aggressive, all pull straight back into line.
🌪️ WIND — Thunder (Turnover Reaction)

Physical and mental change of speed and intent. The moment we win the ball back — WIND. We stop their attack in the first 20 seconds. Thunder attack on turnover ball. Go immediately, with intensity, before they can reorganise.

Scrum Defence — LHSIf 8 runs blindside: 6 on 1st man, 8 covers inside, 11 connects with 6 on 2nd man, 15 covers. If they come openside: 7 takes 1st man, 9 on the 10. 10 stays square, links both 7 and 9. Near-side lock and 6 on the fold.

Breakdown Principles

One bullet = one kill · Clean out accuracy · Support line · Check line

One Bullet = One Kill. Every cleanout must be accurate and decisive. Never waste a body at the breakdown — arrive with a clear purpose and execute it completely.
💥 Arrival Order — Roles
BC
Ball Carrier. Most carries go at least 1m beyond the defender. Ball grip — squeeze before contact. Ball position — present back toward own players. TK speed — squeeze the ball on the way down.
2
2nd Man — take space or take the threat. Read immediately on arrival. Is the ball secure? Is there a threat to the ball? Decide in the first step and commit fully. One bullet.
3
3rd Man — inside gate. Always. This is non-negotiable. Secure the inside gate before anything else. One kill. No exceptions — the gate must be locked before 3rd man considers any other option.
4
4th Man — make a decision. Is the breakdown secure? Threat on the edge? Space to exploit on the next phase? Be the smart player. Read the full picture and react — this is your moment.
📐 Support Line — Check Line

Support players must run a check line — not a straight line to the ball carrier. The check line gives the ability to either take a pass or support at the breakdown immediately.

Take DownIf the ball carrier is going to be tackled, the support player must be on the right line to arrive at the breakdown immediately. Not reacting — anticipating. The check line is the discipline that makes this possible.
Inside Gate PrioritySecure the inside gate before anything else. The gate is the most important position at every breakdown. A secured inside gate stops the opposition from winning the ball — an unsecured gate costs us possession.
Ball grip · Ball position · TK speed. These three fundamentals apply to the ball carrier on every single carry. No exceptions — every time.

Kicking Game

Zone-based decisions · Kick pressure · Taiho kick situation

🦵 Kick Strategy by Zone
🟡
Gold Zone — Kick to Score. Grubber into space, cross-kick to winger, chip over the line. Only kick if it creates a clear try-scoring opportunity. Ball in hand is usually better.
🟢
Green Zone — Kick to Gain. Box kick to contest, cross-field, chip behind the line. Create territory and pressure. Contest immediately — never let them catch clean.
🟠
Amber Zone — Kick to Retrieve. Box kick from 9, contest high. Apply pressure to their backfield. Win the lineout from their kick return. Kick with chase — never kick and watch.
🔴
Red Zone — Kick to Relieve. Kick long and contest their lineout. Exit own 22 as fast as possible. Never kick out on the full. Minimise rucks spent in own half.
⚠️ Taiho — Kick Pressure Situation

Designed to pressure the 10 who sits back in the pocket to kick. Our 9 joins the defensive line but works independent of the line — aims at the right foot of the kicker to pressure the kick.

Setup9 joins the defensive line but D2 is on his outside shoulder. 9 aims directly at the kicking foot of their 10. 8 drops back with both wings and 15 to create a deep safety net. 13 can start to move back once 10 drops into the pocket and receives the ball.
Brilliant Simple KickingLHS set piece — Sora from 10. RHS set piece — Katame from 9. Slow ball — Kiji X Shuttle. Kick return: LHS to 10, RHS to 9. Simple, clear decisions — no hesitation.
