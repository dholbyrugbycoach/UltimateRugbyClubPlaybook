1·3·3·1 Shape

Organisation · Clarity · Mismatches · Ball Always Moving

The shape is our framework. It gives us options, creates width, and keeps the defence guessing. 2 Forwards on Edge. Forward pods move North/South. Connection is everything.
🔷 1·3·3·1 Field Shape
🔷 1 — Edge

Single threat (11 or 14 + 1 forward). Designed to attack the edge. Force the defence to commit wide players.

🔷 3 — Left Pod

Three-player forward pod. Set on outside shoulder of D3. Inside tip (limited). Outside tip when D3/D4 bites. North/South movement.

🔷 3 — Right Pod

Mirror of left pod. 10 connects this group. 12 and 13 provide the bridge. React to where space appears.

Pod Shape

BLUE ball · WHITE ball · GOLD option · Non-negotiable alignment

🔵 BLUE Ball — Off 9

Played off 9 with attached player (GOLD option). First player in pod sets with hips aligned to outside shoulder of Defender 3.

Player 1 — First in pod. Set on outside shoulder D3. Receive and make ground.
Player 2 — Inside tip (limited use). First to breakdown (SPIKE).
Player 3 — Outside tip. Play when D3/D4 bites to open space. Second to breakdown (LOCK).
GOLD — Attached player behind Player 1. Play when defence is narrow and passive. Call FIRE to demand the ball.
Non-negotiable: Set up on outside shoulder of defender. Always.
⚪ WHITE Ball — Off 10

Played off 10 with attached player (GOLD option). Depth needed — start run when 10 catches.

10 to 1 — 10 passes to pod leader (1) who tips to 2 or 3 if space opens from defence.
10 to GOLD — If defence is blitzing hard, 1/2/3 flood through. Or option to get into space.
Zone use — Play 22-to-22 in 1st/2nd phase to get ball to edge, especially against narrow defence.
🟡 GOLD — Queen Option

Attached player behind the pod leader. Used when defence is narrow and passive — they are defending the pod, not the space behind. If you want the ball: call FIRE. To hold and carry: HOLD.

Phase Plays

Decision-making by zone · Strike plays · Patience plays

🟡 Gold Zone — Strike Phase
1
Ruthless mentality. Must come away with points. Strike in first 3 phases.
2
Target space, weak seams, weak defenders. Line break or score — nothing else is acceptable.
3
Maximum 3 pod carries, then attack space or kick to score (BLACK). No more than that.
🟢 Green Zone — Build Phase
1
WHITE ball first phase. Create width, find and play the best space. Multi-option attack — GOLD options.
2
Strike first 3 phases — aim to score or line break. Go forward by identifying and playing to space.
3
Only kick to score (BLACK). Focus entirely on ball retention and forward momentum.
🟡 Amber Zone — Territory First
1
WHITE ball first phase. Apply pressure then relieve through territory if needed.
2
Strike in first 3 phases. If no dominance after 3 — BLACK (kick) to exert pressure and retrieve.
3
Run / BLACK / pass from set piece to find spaces. Apply pressure — retrieve.
🔴 Red Zone — Exit Fast
1
Relieve pressure through territory or run into space immediately.
2
Minimise time in own 22. Minimise rucks spent in own half. BLACK long and contest.
3
If running — must go forward. No lateral plays. Get out of the danger zone.

Set Piece — From Lineout

Back alignment · Receiving positions · Zone-based first phase options

9 positioning: In 7-man lineouts — 9 sweeps. In shorter lineouts — 9 may enter lineout as extra body. Align before the throw.
📋 Backs Alignment
9: Receives from jumper or sweeps in 7-man. Sets tempo of delivery. No telegraphing.
10: Aligns based on zone of lineout win. Deeper for back ball — flatter for front ball. First decision maker.
12: Inside centre connection — links 9/10 to outside. GOLD option player when attached to pod.
13: Holds width, threats to the outside. Identifies if 11/14 have space before the ball arrives.
11/14: Edges of the shape. React to the picture. Always a threat — always scanning for space.
15: Floating depth. Comes into the line or holds as kicking option. Must be on feet and ready.
⚡ Zone-Based First Phase Options
FOX (Front ball)
Fast delivery. 9 attacks the line immediately. BLUE ball — pod on 9. Quick strike before defence sets.
HAWK (Middle ball)
Best platform. Most options available. WHITE ball creates width. GOLD option most viable from middle ball.
BEAR (Back ball)
GREEN — go wide fast. Full backs width available. 9 has time — use the space to the short side also.
DRIVE delivery
Backs hold width and stay wide while forwards build the maul. 9 is last man out. 10 controls timing of exit.

📋 Backs Scrum Alignment Rules
1
10 reads the call first. Alignment depth and width set based on delivery type. Deeper = more time. Flatter = more pressure.
2
LAGER: 10 can take the ball early. Wide backs available. GOLD option very viable — defence often flat.
3
AMBER: 10 holds until ball exits forward drive. Identifies whether to attack short or go wide when ball arrives.
4
All backs: Stay on-side, stay connected, stay scanning. The scrum is our reset — use the time to read the defence shape.

Defence Structure

Line · Blitz · Fold · Connection — backs responsibilities

🛡️ STORM — Blitz Line
1
Load up — stay connected and ready. Eyes on ball. React to 9's body position.
2
Go hard together — 5m square, fast off the line. Never go early. Never go alone.
3
Hunt from outside in. Force ball carrier inside. Take away the space on the edge.
🌊 TIDE — Hold Line
1
Stay connected. Inside and outside players — communicate constantly. Commentate what you see.
2
Contain and control. Don't rush. Force the opposition to make the decision under pressure.
3
Hold width — 13 and 14 do not squeeze inside. Edge cover is critical. 15 reads the kicks.
❄️ FROST — CBA Fold System
C
1st to Arrive
Pushes wide. Checks A but immediately takes C position. Focus: 1st threat wide.
B
2nd to Arrive
Fills mid-channel B position. Focus: 9 / 1st receiver.
A
3rd to Arrive
Fills closest to ruck — A position. Focus: 9 / ball carrier.
Set square — C leads line speed when ball is played. C out to face up. All players set square before stepping off the line.

Kicking Game

BLACK call · Zone-based decisions · Types · Contest

BLACK = any kick. The type of kick is the 10's decision, informed by the zone and the picture in front of them.
⚫ BLACK — Kick Types
Box Kick — High, contestable. Used in amber zone to retrieve. Chasers must be in position early.
Cross-kick — Into a wide open winger. Gold zone only. Must be accurate.
Grubber — Low kick into space behind the line. Short to medium range in gold zone.
Chip / Chip & Chase — Over the first defender into space. Requires speed of thought and immediate chase.
Long Kick — Territorial exit. Red zone. Kick long, contest the lineout. Don't kick out on the full.
🎯 Kick Contest Responsibilities
1
Chasers must be in position before the kick. 10 checks chase is set before kicking. No kick without pressure.
2
15 reads the opposition kick. First decision — catch and attack or kick return? Call it early so everyone can react.
3
Do not kick without pressure on the catcher. They will counter attack and punish loose kicks from deep.
4
Contest every exit. Speed of chase makes their kicker rush. Force errors. Every kick is an opportunity.

Restarts — Back Roles

Kick-off attack · Reception positioning · Backing the catcher

⚡ Kick-Off Attack — Backs
1
9, 10, 12 — shape behind kick. Be ready to receive the ball immediately if opposition fumble or kick returns short.
2
11 and 14 — chase hard. Primary chasers on a contestable kick. Chase the channels.
3
13 and 15 — hold depth. Cover any kicked return. Read the catcher's decision and support immediately.
🛡️ Kick-Off Reception — Backs
1
15 primary catcher. Calls early — catch and attack, or kick return? Communicates to 9 and 10 immediately.
2
Backs support line. All backs must be on a check line — not a straight line to 15. Able to receive or support breakdown.
3
10 calls the first phase. As soon as 15 catches, 10 calls BLUE/WHITE/GREEN. The shape reacts immediately.
