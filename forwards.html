Lineout Principles

10 core rules — always operate faster than the opposition

Core Standard: Always operate with tempo — always quicker than the opposition. Fast to the lineout, fast jumps and lifts, fast delivery, fast to the next phase.
📋 10 Laws of Our Lineout
1
Always operate with tempo. Faster than the opposition every single time. Fast to set, fast to jump, fast to deliver, fast to next phase.
2
Set a big gap and maintain it. Manage this ourselves — never rely on the referee. Jump and lift straight down our line.
3
Clear, loud calls every time. Assess the fast option first at every lineout. Always offer an option to the hooker at the front.
4
Pre-call on the way. Know your role before you arrive. Hooker gets to the lineout first. Bookends set the gap, rest bring the tempo.
5
Everybody works off the ball. Dummy and move to distract the opposition even when the ball is not coming to you. Sell it.
6
Finish every lift. Put the jumper out of the line, away from the defence. The lift ends when the ball is delivered clean.
7
Precise delivery every time. Damaging deliveries stop opposition competing in the air. Every player protects 9 on a pass delivery.
8
Group for drive, spread for tempo. Formation changes the picture. Read the call and adjust position before the throw.
9
Gold standard — I Frame. Square body, strong frame in the air. Legs together, arms locked, present a clean target at the top.
10
Last man is the caller. Caller controls the tempo of the whole lineout. Clear communication wins the lineout before the throw leaves the hand.

📋 Delivery Types
PASS — Pass to 9
Jumper receives and delivers cleanly to 9 off the top. Every player around the 9 must protect if necessary.
DRIVE — Maul
Ball retained in contact. SUMMIT lands → RIDGE drives → RIVER controls ball → BOULDER clears → PLAINS fills. Build the platform first.
FAST — Quick lineout
Assess opportunity before every lineout. If available — always take it. Fast lineout beats any defence.
🚫 Cancel Calls
If the original call is compromised, the caller shouts CHECK to reset. Hooker holds, re-calls with new zone and delivery. Stay calm — no rush.

Lifting Technique

Jumper · Front lifter · Back lifter · I Frame standard

⬆️ Jumper
1
Jump square to our lineout. Never jump into the line.
2
Strong, straight body in the air. Keep legs together. I Frame.
3
Arms locked out, present a clean target. Commit to the ball fully.
4
If selling a dummy — commit to the movement. Half-hearted sells don't work.
🙌 Front Lifter (RIDGE Front)
1
Hands on front of legs. Thumbs on the inside.
2
Be square to the jumper. Keep them out of the line — never let them drift in.
3
Drive up and hold — squeeze as tight as possible at the top of the lift.
4
Place the jumper out of the lineout after delivery.
🙌 Back Lifter (RIDGE Back)
1
Keep driving feet towards the jumper. Never take them back out.
2
Start under the glutes. Catch on the hamstrings of the jumper as they rise.
3
Both bodies squeezed as tight as possible to the jumper at the top.
4
Control the landing — lower with the jumper, don't just drop.

Lineout Defence

Steal the ball or give the lowest quality possession possible — 6 stages

🛡️ 6-Stage Defensive Sequence
1
Get set first. Get correct numbers fast. Set immediately in correct formation. Close the gap — squeeze their space. Get off the ground fast with jumpers as high as possible.
2
Attack delivery in the air. Jumper competes for the ball. If they catch — attack the jumper's hands hard. Make them bring the ball to their chest. Lifters push our jumper into theirs.
3
Disrupt on the way down. Jumper uses their body to hit into opposition jumper as they come down. Take their space. Lifters lower body height quickly — TK Speed.
4
Attack jumper and lifters on landing. All players lower body height — TK Speed. Try to dislodge from their lifters. Attack the arms so they cannot deliver to their 9.
5
Destroy their driving platform. Jumper attacks jumper. Lifters get inside attacking lifters and split them away — lift legs and drive them away. If they seal off — hit their lower back, pick a leg, twist them away.
6
Pressure their 9's delivery. If they deliver to their scrum half — attack him. Make him rush his pass or kick. If they win at the front — one player fights through the lineout to reach their 9.

Scrum Principles

Unmoveable · Launch Pad · Mindset

🦏
UNMOVEABLE

Like a rhino — solid, strong, connected as one. No individual collapses. We go together.

🚀
LAUNCH PAD

A dominant platform for our attack every time. The backs should receive clean, quick ball.

🧠
MINDSET

Dominant match day scrum. Every engagement. Technical superiority. Win before the ball comes in.

⚙️ Core Scrum Rules
1
Set low and tight every time. Win the hit — this is the hooker's responsibility to initiate. Forward and down — engage and move opposition feet immediately.
2
Push straight is the default. If under pressure — stick together as one. Never let opposition separate the front row. The vertical axe is the reference point.
3
Vary delivery speed. Never be predictable. Drive scrum first, then deliver. Hook when ready — never on 9's call. Vary from LAGER to STOUT to keep opposition guessing.
4
8 hit and chase. Number 8 engages as a unit then chases delivery — sequence 1, 2, 3 on signal. Read the call and be ready to carry before the ball is in.

📋 How Calls Work

The call is made before the scrum engages. Every forward must know the call and their role before Crouch is called by the referee.

LAGER — Clean strike, 9 takes the put-in side. Guards clear their channels immediately.
STOUT — 8 takes the ball, drives hard off the base. 9 supports immediately.
AMBER — Drive first, then ball exits from under the scrum. 9 waits for it.
PORTER — Collective wheel. The call to initiate wheel timing comes from hooker.

Maul Roles

Terrain theme · 5 roles · Every player has a job before the ball lands

🏔️ SUMMIT — The Jumper

Launched upward — triple extension on landing. Establish the anchor point of the maul. Land with intent, pull defenders to you.

⛰️ RIDGE — The Lifters

Turn on the way down and drive forward — the pointed attack that breaks the defence. Become the spearhead of the maul. Head down, legs driving.

🌊 RIVER — The Ball Carrier

Protects the ball and controls distribution. Right height, deliver cleanly when the time is right. Keep the ball alive — the RIVER flows forward.

🪨 BOULDER — Clearers

Shoulder on to bums or into gaps. Cut through — create separation and distance between opposition defenders. BOULDER clears the path.

🌾 PLAINS — Support

Fill space, keep the attack loaded. Anticipate changes of direction, cover breakaway threats, and ensure the maul never runs out of numbers.

★ Key Detail

RIDGE closer to touchline — head inside. Further from touchline — head out. Hooker becomes a BOULDER when throwing to front of lineout for speed and safety.

Maul Execution

Platform · Drive · 4th man · Ball carrier · Arriving players

📋 Building the Platform — Arrival Order
1
SUMMIT lands. Must protect the most exposed RIDGE lifter. If ball won at front or middle — back lifter. If at back — front lifter.
2
4th man role. Can go in front of lifter (if opposition attack aggressively) or behind (if driving on a short lineout). Protects the exposed lifter.
3
RIVER rips the ball. Head on the infield side of the jumper. SUMMIT anchors to RIVER by binding on his back — strong against any attempts to sack.
4
Next two arriving players go either side of RIVER and drive infield. As they get past, they attempt to bind together to make the maul even tighter. SUMMIT binds to these two.
5
Last player (PLAINS) takes the ball from RIVER. As often as possible — this should be the hooker. If closer to front, hooker arrives earlier; back row takes the ball instead.
6
Keep the ball carrier on the ball. Players rejoining the maul hit in past him — leaving him at the back with the ball. Only transfer again if he is exposed too far forward.

Breakdown Principles

One bullet, one kill · Cleanout accuracy · Arrival order · Support line

One Bullet = One Kill. Every cleanout must be accurate and decisive. Arrive with a purpose and execute it completely. Never waste a body.
⚡ Arrival Order — Roles
BC
Ball Carrier: Go at least 1m beyond the defender. Squeeze the ball tight. Ball grip, position — everything before contact.
2
2nd Man: Take space or take the threat. Read immediately — is ball secure or is there a threat? Decide and commit. Call SPIKE.
3
3rd Man: Secure the inside gate. Non-negotiable — always. No exceptions. Call LOCK.
4
4th Man: Make a decision. Breakdown secure? Threat on edge? Space to exploit? Be the smart player — read and react.
🎯 Fundamentals
Ball Grip
Squeeze the ball tight before contact. Never go to ground without full control of the ball.
Ball Position
Place ball back towards your own players. Make life easy for 9. Present clean.
TK Speed
Lower body height immediately on arrival. Slow arrivals cost possession. Low and fast — every time.
Support Line — Check Line
Run a check line — not a straight line. Gives you the option to receive a pass or arrive at the breakdown. The inside gate is always the priority.

Restarts — Forward Roles

Kick-off responsibilities · Aerial contest · Protection · Support

⚡ Kick-Off Attack
1
Aggression and linespeed. Every forward must be hard off the line. No soft chasing.
2
Compete if contestable. Left/right wing and back row are the primary contesters. Hit the air hard.
3
Win the first collision. If they catch — the first contact must be won by us. Arrive with power.
4
Pressure exit kicks. Make their kicker rush. Speed of chase forces decision errors.
🛡️ Kick-Off Reception
1
Protect the catcher. Never allow a straight run to contest the ball. Get a body in front of chasers.
2
Urgency off the ball. Movement to support positions before the ball lands. Anticipate, don't react.
3
Gather loose ball. Players near the catcher ready to secure any loose ball immediately.
4
Decide early. Full lift or no lift — call before the ball is in the air. Commit to the decision.
